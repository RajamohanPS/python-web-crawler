An underlying aspect of all my decisions I take in my life is the passion for creation that makes maybe small but exciting change.
I have always aspired to create something that can influence.

Post my BTech and Masters from MDI, Gurgaon, I started working as a Marketing Manager at a Test Prep organization.
I had hoped to work in close proximity with the students. However, even though the work was fruitful, my objective wasnt being fulfilled. ...
Thus, I quit.

My mentor then was building an organization with the purpose of imparting employability skills to the less privileged. When he offered me the opportunity to head the business, I got very excited.
The feeling of successfully transforming lives was truly amazing! Eventually, the firm started working successfully in close quarters with the govt and I realized that its time to start something of my own.

I found a similar passion and drive of creating something substantial in my two school friends and together, we started GENESIS AI in 2014.

When we think of Artificial Intelligence, we think about Silicon Valley and first world problems. We resolved to change this and explore it in the Indian context.

Now, we, at Genesis, power manufacturing processes, extract information and convert images into analyzable data. As we move ahead, we are doing more research, widening our audience and executing exciting solutions.

Currently, we are working with banks and manufacturing industries to automate the services. Our main motivation behind this is to enable smooth functioning of operations even in times like now when human interaction is a no-no.

In these 6 years, weve come a long way and we havent even touched the tip of the iceberg. Throughout it all, I am happy to note that the passion is still burning. The drive to change is still the one behind the wheels.

If I were to advice someone, I would implore you to work in an industry of your liking, for some time so that you gain domain knowledge.
I would also suggest keeping your spending at a minimum and saving as much as you can.
Starting a company entails many uncertainties. With a cushion and low cost of living, it becomes easy to sail over the uncertainities.

Creativity without strategy is just an art.

I am Dhriti Goyal.
I hail from a small town in Rajasthan. I finished my schooling with Science stream from a boarding school. Pretty soon, I realized that this isnt my calling and thus, decided to pursue Bachelors in Mass Communication.

...

During my undergrad, I was the typical nerd. I sat on the 1st bench, never missed any classes and was loved by all the teachers. After my 2 month internship, I realised that I wasnt really ready for a job at that point. So, I chose to do my Masters in Advertising with a Major in Brand Mgt. at Symbiosis.

After completing my Masters, Happay appointed me. Its been a journey from a young startup to a Soonicorn now. That too in just 4 years. I have seen the company take a 360-degree change. When I started, we were situated just in Bangalore and had just 50 clients. Now, weve around 6k clients and weve an office in US too. The shift was surprising for me but like a good Marketer, I adapted.

Like every other aspiring marketer, I came into this field with the expectations that Ill start building advertising campaigns and working with brands, right away. The reality struck when I was handed Excels and PPTs to work with. Gradually, I realized that every aspect constitutes an important part.

I have worked in different types of marketing and have even executed and mentored events, alone, under Event Marketing. Now, with lockdown, I am trying my capabilities in other horizons like virtual events, email marketing, user engagement and so on.

If I were to give any advice to all marketing enthusiasts, it would be to always
1. Be aware of your organisations Northstar metric and follow it.
2. Keep your number game strong. No matter which part of marketing you handle.
3. SMarketing is real and very important.
4. Know your customers. Its basic but mostly forgotten. The difference between a good Marketer and the best is the ability to adapt quicker to the agility the marketing world offers.

I graduated from NIT Trichy with B.Tech in Chemical Engineering. Then I joined Columbia University for my Masters in Mgt. Science and Engineering in 2017.
My major was a cross between engineering and business, so I studied courses like analytics, data science, and consulting.
I came to the U.S. wanting to be a consultant. However, when that didnt work out, I ended up meeting with a Product Manager from Google. The 1 hour talk I had with him in a coffee shop made me ponder ...upon this new venture as a possible career option. I saw a lot of parallels between both career paths, so I began hunting for Product Manager roles.
After many failed interviews, I got my offer to work at Salesforce on April 2nd, 2018. After a few months, I got a return offer to join as an APM in the data science and security team. On my first day as a full-time employee, I assumed that a few more days would be enough to adjust and adapt.
Alas, that wasnt the case. After 3 weeks of struggle, I realized that I had barely scratched the surface. It took at least 3 months to get up to speed and understand the area I was working on. I scheduled interviews with many engineers from sister-teams. It felt like everyone gave me a piece of the puzzle, yet I couldnt see the big picture. During this time, my manager told me something that I still remember. He said, Its okay to want to know where the resources are. But, you also have to be okay with the fact that it might not be in any one place.
As an out-of-college product manager, I soon realized I cant contribute much in terms of road map and direction in a niche field like cyber security as everyone around me had more than a decade of experience. But, I can contribute in coming up with next steps, finding the right people, gathering data, and going back to my team to brainstorm further.
My advice to everyone who is a new Product Manager is to not fretter over the unknown. Rather, do your best to talk to your users and collect feedback and data. Eventually, the unknown will become a new normal.

The journey from a Meerut girl to a graduate student studying at Columbia University, New York while working part time was an intense one.

...

Because of my persistence, determination and the presence of mentors like him who provided me with their unwavering support, I was able to form Associate Product Managers Club (APMC) that is a PM-exclusive community that helps accelerate ones product management career.

Throughout my journey, I learned that in order to achieve your dreams, it is important to SAP i.e. Support, Adapt and Persist.

The process is simple, be unapologetic to give or ask for support, customize the success according to your own story and perform everything with a passion so strong, it burns like fire.

To support my family mentally, I had to leave my job and stay at home while all my friends were living their dream. As days passed I engaged myself in social outreach gatherings and found my solace in helping underprivileged people around me in every possible way. Being aware of my innate passion for social work my father introduced me to a local NGO of his friend and I started working there as a volunteer, managing everything family, social work, and peoples taunts of being jobless. Despite initial hurdles, doing things close to my heart kept me going everyday stronger.

As a volunteer, I distributed essentials to the needy and participated in hygiene and nutrition awareness camps in the slums. Later, I started organising events like mental health awareness week, womens day and so on. I enrolled myself with many NGOs and social welfare committees to work relentlessly. My hard work got recognition and drew applause from prominent people in the NGO communities.

Today, I am a proud recipient of the Maharashtra district youth award, Dada Saheb Phalke yatra award, Prerana award, and Swami Vivekanand national youth award. I was also felicitated by Education Minister Mr. Vinod Tawde for my exemplary social work. I have also extended my helping hand to international communities and I am honored to be invited for an UN-led climate change conference called CAMP 2030 happening in NewYork.
